Designing an MGUI panel

Overview

This guide will show you how to create your first 3D holographic user interface using MGUI, our library of 3D UI elements.

By the end of this guide, you will be able to:

  • Use the MGUI.Canvas prefab as the foundation for your UI elements
  • Create transparent MGUI.Panels
  • Create MGUI.Buttons that trigger an action when pressed

Requirements

  • Unity for Windows
  • Meta SDK Installer
  • One pair of Meta glasses

This tutorial also assumes that you have already completed the following:

  • Hello MetaWorld

Steps

Setting up

  1. Plug in and set up your Meta glasses
  2. Run Unity
  3. Open the 'Meta' project
  4. Open your 'Hello MetaWorld' scene
  5. Set the 'Hello MetaWorld App' GameObject to inactive in the Inspector window to hide our previous app
    1. Select the 'Hello MetaWorld App' GameObject in the Hierarchy window
    2. Click on the checkbox next to the 'MetaCube' text at the top of the Inspector window
  6. Create an empty GameObject at position (0, 0, 0)
    1. GameObject > Create Empty
    2. Modify the values in the Inspector window so that the position is (0, 0, 0)
  7. Rename the empty GameObject to 'Hello MGUI App'
  8. Add the MetaAppObject script to your 'Hello MGUI App' GameObject
    1. Select the 'Hello MGUI App' GameObject in the Hierarchy window
    2. Click Add Component at the bottom of the Inspector window
    3. Type in 'MetaAppObject' and click on the search result to add it

Adding the MGUI.Canvas and MGUI.Panel panel

  1. Click and drag the MGUI.Canvas prefab from the Project View to your 'Hello MGUI App' GameObject in the Hierarchy window at position (0, 0, 0.4)
    1. Search for MGUI.Canvas in the search box at the top-right of the Project View OR navigate to Assets > Meta > MetaSpace > MGUI > Resources > MGUI Prefabs > MGUI.Canvas
    2. Click and drag the MGUI.Canvas prefab from the Project View to your 'Hello MGUI App' GameObject in the Hierarchy window
    3. Verify that the position of the 'MGUI.Canvas' GameObject in the Inspector window is (0, 0, 0.4)
  2. Click and drag the MGUI.Panel prefab from the Project View to your 'MGUI.Canvas' GameObject in the Hierarchy window
    1. Search for MGUI.Panel in the search box at the top-right of the Project View OR navigate to Assets > Meta > MetaSpace > MGUI > Resources > MGUI Prefabs > MGUI.Panel
    2. Click and drag the MGUI.Panel prefab from the Project View to your 'MGUI.Canvas' GameObject in the Hierarchy window
Adding the MGUI.Panel

Adding a MetaCube to 'MGUI.Panel'

  1. Click and drag the MetaCube prefab from the Project View to your 'MGUI.Panel' GameObject in the Hierarchy window at position (0, 75, 0) with scale (200, 200, 200)
    1. Search for MetaCube in the search box at the top-right of the Project View OR navigate to Assets > Meta > MetaSpace > Resources > _Prefabs > MetaPrimitives > MetaCube
    2. Click and drag the MetaCube prefab from the Project View to your 'MGUI.Panel' GameObject in the Hierarchy window
    3. Modify the values in the Inspector window so that the position is (0, 20, 0) and the scale is (150, 150, 150)
  2. Set the 'MetaCube' GameObject to inactive in the Inspector window to hide the cube
    1. Select the 'MetaCube' GameObject in the Hierarchy window
    2. Click on the checkbox next to the 'MetaCube' text at the top of the Inspector window so that it becomes unchecked
Deactivating MetaCube

Adding a button to 'MGUI.Panel'

  1. Click and drag the MGUI.Button prefab from the Project View to your 'MGUI.Panel' GameObject in the Hierarchy window at position (290, -335, 0)
    1. Search for MGUI.Button in the search box at the top-right of the Project View OR navigate to Assets > Meta > MetaSpace > MGUI > MGUI Prefabs > MGUI.Button
    2. Click and drag the MGUI.Button prefab from the Project View to your 'MGUI.Panel' GameObject in the Hierarchy window
    3. Modify the values in the Inspector window so that the position is (290, -335, 0)
  2. Add the 'MetaCube' GameObject to the list of On Click () event targets in the Inspector window of the 'MGUI.Button' GameObject
    1. Select the 'MGUI.Button' GameObject in the Hierarchy window
    2. Find the Button (Script) component of the 'MGUI.Button' GameObject in the Inspector window
    3. Click the '+' button under the On Click () section of the Button (Script) component
    4. Click and drag the 'MetaCube' GameObject from the Hierarchy window to the new 'None' box in the On Click () section
  3. Change the On Click () event trigger on the 'MGUI.Button' GameObject to make the 'MetaCube' GameObject become activated
    1. Click the trigger drop-down box ("No Function" by default) next to the 'MetaCube' item in the On Click () section of the 'MGUI.Button' Inspector window
    2. Select GameObject > SetActive (bool) in the menu
      Adding an MGUI.Button
    3. Click on the checkbox next to the 'MetaCube' item in On Click () so that it becomes checked
      Checkbox to activate MetaCube when button is pressed
  4. Save the scene and click the Play button to run the scene
  5. Touch the 'Button' with your finger. It should make the 'MetaCube' appear in the middle of the panel
Making a cube appear in Game View

Conclusion

Congratulations, you've made a simple MGUI interface with a button that triggers a 'MetaCube' GameObject! All MGUI interface elements (eg. panels) should be placed within an MGUI.Canvas, and the elements associated with each panel (eg. buttons) should be placed as a child of their respective panels.
