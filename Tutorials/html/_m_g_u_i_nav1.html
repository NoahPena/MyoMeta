Designing MGUI navigation

Making usable navigation

Overview

This guide will show you how to create a simple navigational interface with multiple panels using MGUI.

By the end of this guide, you will be able to:

  • Create a simple navigation system between two panels

Requirements

  • Unity for Windows
  • Meta SDK Installer
  • One pair of Meta glasses

This tutorial also assumes that you have already completed the following:

  • Designing an MGUI panel

Steps

Setting up

  1. Plug in and set up your Meta glasses
  2. Run Unity
  3. Open the 'Meta' project
  4. Open your 'Hello MetaWorld' scene
  5. Set the 'Hello MGUI App' GameObject to inactive in the Inspector window to hide our previous app
    1. Select the 'Hello MGUI App' GameObject in the Hierarchy window
    2. Click on the checkbox next to the 'MetaCube' text at the top of the Inspector window
  6. Create an empty GameObject at position (0, 0, 0)
    1. GameObject > Create Empty
    2. Modify the values in the Inspector window so that the position is (0, 0, 0)
  7. Rename the empty GameObject to 'MGUI Nav App'
  8. Add the MetaAppObject script to your 'MGUI Nav App' GameObject
    1. Select the 'MGUI Nav App' GameObject in the Hierarchy window
    2. Click Add Component at the bottom of the Inspector window
    3. Type in 'MetaAppObject' and click on the search result to add it
Adding the MGUI Nav App GameObject

Adding the MGUI.Canvas and MGUI.Panel panels

  1. Click and drag the MGUI.Canvas prefab from the Project View to your 'MGUI Nav App' GameObject in the Hierarchy window at position (0, 0, 0.4)
    1. Search for MGUI.Canvas in the search box at the top-right of the Project View OR navigate to Assets > Meta > MetaSpace > MGUI > Resources > MGUI Prefabs > MGUI.Canvas
    2. Click and drag the MGUI.Canvas prefab from the Project View to your 'MGUI Nav App' GameObject in the Hierarchy window
    3. Verify that the position of the 'MGUI.Canvas' GameObject in the Inspector window is (0, 0, 0.4)
  2. Click and drag the MGUI.Panel prefab from the Project View to your 'MGUI.Canvas' GameObject in the Hierarchy window
    1. Search for MGUI.Panel in the search box at the top-right of the Project View OR navigate to Assets > Meta > MetaSpace > MGUI > Resources > MGUI Prefabs > MGUI.Panel
    2. Click and drag the MGUI.Panel prefab from the Project View to your 'MGUI.Canvas' GameObject in the Hierarchy window
  3. Rename the 'MGUI.Panel' GameObject to 'MGUI.Panel 1' in the Inspector window
    1. Select the 'MGUI.Panel' in the Hierarchy window
    2. Type in 'MGUI.Panel 1' into the name box at the top of the Inspector window and press enter
      Renaming MGUI.Panel
  4. Change the text section of the 'Text (Script)' component of the 'MGUI.Text Panel Title' GameObject from 'Panel' to 'Panel 1'
    1. Expand both the 'MGUI.Panel 1' GameObject and its 'MGUI.Image Panel Titlebar' child GameObject in the Hierarchy window by clicking the triangles next to the name of each GameObject
    2. Select the 'MGUI.Text Panel Title' GameObject
    3. Type in 'Panel 1' into the 'Text' box of the 'Text' component in the Inspector window
      Changing the Label of MGUI.Panel 1
  5. Click and drag another MGUI.Panel prefab from the Project View to your 'MGUI.Canvas' GameObject in the Hierarchy window
  6. Rename the 'MGUI.Panel' GameObject to 'MGUI.Panel 2' in the Inspector window
  7. Change the text section of the 'Text (Script)' component of 'MGUI.Text Panel Title' GameObject from 'MGUI.Panel' to 'MGUI.Panel 2'
  8. Set the 'MGUI.Panel 2' GameObject to inactive in the Inspector window to hide the panel
    1. Select the 'MGUI.Panel 2' GameObject
    2. Click on the checkbox next to the 'MGUI.Panel 2' text in the Inspector window
Deactivating MGUI.Panel 2

Adding a button to 'MGUI.Panel 1'

  1. Click and drag the MGUI.Button prefab from the Project View to your 'MGUI.Panel 1' GameObject in the Hierarchy window at position (290, -335, 0)
    1. Search for MGUI.Button in the search box at the top-right of the Project View OR navigate to Assets > Meta > MetaSpace > MGUI > MGUI Prefabs > MGUI.Button
  2. Rename the 'MGUI.Button' GameObject to 'MGUI.Button Nav' in the Inspector window
  3. Add the 'MGUI.Panel 2' GameObject to the list of On Click () event targets in the Inspector window of the 'MGUI.Button Nav' GameObject
    1. Select the 'MGUI.Button Nav' GameObject in the Hierarchy window
    2. Find the Button (Script) component of the 'MGUI.Button Nav' GameObject in the Inspector window
    3. Click the '+' button under the On Click () section of the Button (Script) component
    4. Click and drag the 'MGUI.Panel 2' GameObject from the Hierarchy window to the new 'None' box in the On Click () section
  4. Change the On Click () event trigger on the 'MGUI.Button Nav' GameObject to make the 'MGUI.Panel 2' GameObject become activated
    1. Click the trigger drop-down box ("No Function" by default) next to the 'MGUI.Panel 2' item in the On Click () section of the 'MGUI.Button Nav' Inspector window
    2. Select GameObject > SetActive (bool) in the menu
    3. Click on the checkbox next to the 'MGUI.Panel 2' item in On Click () so that it becomes checked
  5. Add the 'MGUI.Panel 1' GameObject to the list of On Click () event targets in the Inspector window of the 'MGUI.Button Nav' GameObject
  6. Change the On Click () event trigger on the 'MGUI.Button Nav' GameObject to make the 'MGUI.Panel 1' GameObject become deactivated
    1. Click the trigger drop-down box ("No Function" by default) next to the 'MGUI.Panel 1' item in the On Click () section of the 'MGUI.Button Nav' Inspector window
    2. Select GameObject > SetActive (bool) in the menu
    3. Verify that the checkbox next to the 'MGUI.Panel 1' item in On Click () is unchecked
      Components of the MGUI.Button Nav button
  7. Change the text section of the 'Text (Script)' component the 'MGUI.Button Nav' GameObject from 'Button' to 'Switch Panel'
    1. Expand both the 'MGUI.Button Nav' GameObject and its 'Image' child GameObject in the Hierarchy window by clicking the triangles next to the name of each GameObject
    2. Select the 'Text' GameObject
    3. Type in 'Switch Panel' into the 'Text' box of the 'Text' component in the Inspector window
      Changing the Label of MGUIButtonNav
  8. Save the scene and click the Play button to run the scene
  9. Touch the 'Switch Panel' button with your finger. It should hide the first panel and button and show the second panel
  10. Stop the scene by pushing the Play button again

Saving prefabs for reuse

  1. Navigate to a folder in your Project View that you would like to save the prefab in
  2. Click and drag the 'MGUI.Button Nav' GameObject from the Hierarchy window into your Project View
    1. IMPORTANT: Note that you did NOT click 'Apply' in the Inspector for the changes to the original MGUI.Button prefab. This keeps the original button intact for all the past and future buttons you wish to create from that original simple button template.
    2. Also note that if you only want to make modifications to these prefabs that are specific to the scene you are working in, you can save the scene to have the changes only apply to that scene.
    3. This is mainly useful for GameObjects where you have added scripts or changed properties (eg. text).
Dragging the MGUIButtonNav GameObject

Adding a button to 'MGUI.Panel 2'

  1. Set the 'MGUI.Panel 1' GameObject to inactive in the Inspector window to hide the panel
  2. Set the 'MGUI.Panel 2' GameObject to active in the Inspector window to show the panel
  3. Click and drag the MGUI.Button Nav prefab that you just created from the Project View onto your 'MGUI.Panel 2' GameObject in the Hierarchy window
  4. Add the 'MGUI.Panel 1' GameObject to the list of On Click () event targets in the Inspector window of the 'MGUI.Button Nav' GameObject
  5. Change the On Click () event trigger on the 'MGUI.Button Nav' GameObject to make the 'MGUI.Panel 1' GameObject become activated
  6. Add the 'MGUI.Panel 2' GameObject to the list of On Click () event targets in the Inspector window of the 'MGUI.Button Nav' GameObject
  7. Change the On Click () event trigger on the 'MGUI.Button Nav' GameObject to make the 'MGUI.Panel 2' GameObject become deactivated
  8. Set the 'MGUI.Panel 2' GameObject to inactive in the Inspector window to hide the panel
  9. Set the 'MGUI.Panel 1' GameObject to active in the Inspector window to show the panel
    Adding the MGUIButtonNav to MGUIPanel 2
  1. Save the scene and click the Play button to run the scene
  2. Touch the 'Switch Panel' button with your finger to go back and forth between 'MGUI.Panel 1' and 'MGUI.Panel 2'
MGUI.Panel 1 and Switch Panel button in Game View

Conclusion

Congratulations, you've made a usable navigation interface between holographic panels! You now also know how to save your prefabs if you wish to reuse them in your other projects.
